Glanceability in modern car HUDs

Intro

Together with Jayway Malm√∂ and Uniti I am currently writing my Bachelor's thesis, focusing on how to best design glanceable heads up displays for modern cars.

Process

I am researching how to design Heads Up Displays in cars in a way that maximizes the users' experience, by focusing on the glanceability and ease of use. I have created a test HUD in a VR driving simulator, based on Uniti's HUD. By letting users test different versions of the HUD in the simulator, I will be able to determine how user friendly a car interface based on glanceability is.

The actual VR simulator is developed by two other thesis workers at Jayway, and I've modified the program in order to add a HUD, which accurately simulates the sense of depth and space that the Uniti glanceable hud is based on. The big difference between a classical dashboard and the HUD is that HUD elements are always in the drivers field of vision, which is supposed to allow for a quicker, more glanceable, interaction when the driver needs information. For example, the battery meter and the speedometer are located to the side of the windshield, with a sense of depth that makes it seem like they are floating a few meters in front of the car, while navigation is overlaid onto the road.

The thesis will be complete by May 2018.
